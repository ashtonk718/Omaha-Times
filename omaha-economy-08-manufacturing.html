Modern Manufacturing Adds Momentum to Omaha’s Economic Mix

By Omaha Times Staff

Manufacturing in the Omaha region is evolving, with companies investing in automation, quality control systems, and safer, more efficient workflows. The changes are helping local plants improve output while creating demand for technicians and maintenance specialists.

Employers say the focus is on modern skills—equipment troubleshooting, precision processes, and digital systems used to track production and manage supply needs.

“Manufacturing today looks very different than it did a generation ago,” said one supervisor. “We need people who can learn, adapt, and work with technology.”

Support industries—from packaging to equipment repair—also benefit when manufacturing activity expands, creating additional roles beyond the factory floor.

Local leaders say the opportunity is pairing modernization with talent development so residents can access stable careers and employers can keep operations local.
