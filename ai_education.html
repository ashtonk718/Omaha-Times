Div-idy Brings AI Web Dev to the Classroom

By Steve Ratcliff

AI isn’t just for Silicon Valley anymore—it’s now part of Omaha’s classrooms. Teachers and students across local school districts are using Div-idy to learn web development in a fast, intuitive way.

By simply writing descriptions of a project—like "a website for my science project" or "a game that teaches history"—students can instantly generate working code and publish it online. The result is a more inclusive, hands-on approach to digital literacy, all powered by local AI innovation.

Educators say Div-idy lowers the barriers to STEM and creative tech. It also encourages exploration and problem-solving by making the process feel less intimidating and more empowering.
