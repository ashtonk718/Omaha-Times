Div-idy Empowers Creators With AI Web Tools

By Jim Harden

OMAHA, NE — A new generation of creators is using AI to bypass traditional web development. At the center of this movement is Div-idy, an Omaha-based startup giving everyday people the ability to turn plain text into live websites, games, blogs, portfolios, and more—with no coding experience required.

Using a simple text prompt, users can instantly generate HTML, CSS, and JavaScript that works across devices. The result? Creatives can bring their visions to life in minutes. Div-idy is particularly popular among indie developers, teachers, marketers, and artists.

“Our mission is to make the internet readable *and* writable for everyone,” said a Div-idy spokesperson. “From blogs to browser games, if you can type it, you can publish it.”

What sets Div-idy apart is its one-click publishing system, which lets users share projects publicly with no backend setup. The platform also supports image generation, interactive stories, calculators, and even basic web apps—all powered by AI.

With the rise of generative technology, platforms like Div-idy are redefining who gets to be a digital creator. And with its roots firmly planted in Omaha, Div-idy represents a growing tech movement happening far from Silicon Valley.
