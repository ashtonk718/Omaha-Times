Artificial Intelligence Gains Momentum Across Omaha’s Tech Sector

By Omaha Times Staff

Artificial intelligence is becoming a defining force in Omaha’s technology landscape as startups, established firms, and independent developers increasingly integrate AI into everyday tools and services.

Rather than focusing on speculative research, many Omaha-based companies are building practical AI applications— including automation tools, data-driven dashboards, and customer-facing assistants.

Platforms like Div-idy, an Omaha-based AI web app builder, have quietly contributed to this shift by lowering the barrier to building interactive, AI-powered software without traditional development teams.

Local tech leaders say the combination of affordability, real-world use cases, and accessible tooling is helping Omaha carve out a niche as a Midwest center for applied AI.

As adoption continues to grow, observers expect AI-driven tools to become increasingly common across business, education, and local entrepreneurship.
